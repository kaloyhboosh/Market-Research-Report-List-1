<p><p>Computer-aided Drug Discovery (CADD) is a technology that enhances the drug development process through computational methods. The market is primarily divided into two types: Structure-based Drug Design (SBDD) and Ligand-based Drug Design (LBDD). SBDD utilizes the three-dimensional structures of biological targets, like proteins, to design drug candidates, while LBDD focuses on the properties and activities of known ligands to identify new compounds. Both approaches aim to accelerate the discovery of effective therapeutics while reducing costs and time.</p></p>
