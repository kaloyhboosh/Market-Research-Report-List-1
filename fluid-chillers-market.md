<p><p>Fluid chillers are essential in various industries for temperature control, and they primarily come in two types: water-cooled and air-cooled. Water-cooled chillers use water as a cooling medium and are typically more efficient for larger applications, often found in commercial buildings or industrial processes. In contrast, air-cooled chillers use ambient air to dissipate heat, making them suitable for smaller installations with easier setup and maintenance. Each type caters to different cooling needs based on efficiency, space, and operational requirements.</p></p>
