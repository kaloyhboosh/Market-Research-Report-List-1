<p><p>Dental Glass Ionomer Cements (GIC) are versatile materials used in restorative dentistry, known for their ability to bond chemically to enamel and dentin. They are primarily composed of a glass powder mixed with an acidic solution, providing excellent aesthetic qualities, fluoride release, and biocompatibility. GICs are commonly used for cavity fillings, luting agents, and in pediatric dentistry due to their ease of use and ability to adhere to tooth structure.</p><p>The Dental Glass Ionomer Cements (GIC) Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 11.6% during the forecast period. Additionally, there is a rising demand for aesthetic dental treatments, further boosting GIC usage. Key trends include the development of innovative GIC formulations with improved properties, such as enhanced wear resistance and improved aesthetic outcomes, catering to a broader range of dental applications.
