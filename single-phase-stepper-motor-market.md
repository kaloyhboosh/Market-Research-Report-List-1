<p><ul><li>Variable-reluctance (VR)</li><li>Permanent Magnet (PM)</li><li>Hybrid (HB)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The single-phase stepper motor market consists of three primary types: Variable-reluctance (VR), Permanent Magnet (PM), and Hybrid (HB). VR motors use the principle of variable reluctance to create motion with magnetic fields, making them simpler and cost-effective. PM motors incorporate permanent magnets, providing better torque and efficiency. Hybrid motors combine features of both VR and PM designs, offering enhanced performance and precision control. Each type serves distinct applications based on requirements for torque, speed, and accuracy.</p></p>
